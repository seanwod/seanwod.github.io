Why I Wrote This Paper

Executive Summary

View Summary Slide Deck

The annuity and retirement business is at a pivotal moment where delivering outcomes—not just financial products or digital tools—is what defines success. Our vision is to transform into a digital- and AI-native annuities provider that offers a seamless, best-in-class experience for financial professionals and clients. Today's advisors and their clients demand instant, personalized service—much like they experience in e-commerce and banking—yet our industry remains encumbered by rigid and siloed systems, processes requiring significant human intervention and wait times, and paperwork-heavy workflows. As Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) gives way to Outcomes-as-a-Service (OaaS), expectations have shifted: users no longer want to be given SaaS tools they have to manage—they want intelligent, intuitive experiences that deliver results. Our goal isn't to build another portal for its own sake, but to design a unified digital experience that disappears into the background—resolving issues, guiding decisions, and accelerating outcomes. This strategy outlines how we will bring the advisor and client experience fully in-house, deeply infuse AI across every step of the journey, and modernize our operations and data infrastructure to deliver measurable outcomes—such as faster agent onboarding and transfer times, higher STP rates, increased sales conversion, and improved retention.

Key Initiatives: We will own the end-to-end customer experience via a unified portal for all stakeholders*, rather than relying on third-party interfaces. Through API integration with our core partners (Zinnia for annuities, SS&C for retirement plans), we leverage their back-end engines while retaining control over the user experience and data. We will embed AI capabilities throughout – from 24/7 virtual assistants and AI-driven onboarding, to personalized product recommendations and advisor "co-pilots" – differentiating our service with intelligence and convenience. A unified data platform will underpin these efforts, turning our rich data into actionable insights (e.g. next-best-action models to boost cross-sells, predictive analytics to preempt customer attrition). Internally, our agile operating model supports this vision with its customer-centricity, empowered cross-functional agile teams, iterative incremental delivery, continuous improvement and learning, and an AI governance "Control Tower". This ensures our employees, distribution partners, and BPO providers embrace the new tools and processes in lockstep.

Outcomes and Impact: By executing this strategy, we aim to dramatically shorten processing times (e.g. from weeks to days or hours) through straight-through processing, virtually eliminate Not-In-Good-Order (NIGO) errors via digital workflows, and provide an omnichannel user experience that is consistent, proactive, and personalized. Early industry efforts validate these benefits – for instance, a new digital transfer process (paperless transfers) cut annuity cycle times by 94% (from 18 days to <24 hours), and carriers like Athene have seen electronic submissions soar from <5% to ~90% in a few years by investing in automation. Our AI enhancements will drive efficiency and growth: AI call center bots have already proven to raise productivity by ~70%, and predictive analytics forecasted surrender events months before they occurred at one insurer, unlocking an estimated $5 billion in potential lifetime value. By moving boldly on this roadmap, we will position our company as a market leader – one that is easy to do business with, aligned with industry "digital first" initiatives, and ahead of competitors in harnessing AI. In short, this strategy is our pathway to deliver exceptional customer outcomes (fast, frictionless service; financial confidence) and achieve our business goals of higher revenue, retention, and efficiency. The following white paper details our vision, strategy pillars, and tactical roadmap to becoming the best-in-market digital experience provider in annuity and retirement distribution.

Note: We recognize there may be customers like JPM in the future who may insist that carriers use a specific portal such as Zinnia Live, which we will also be able to accommodate.

Note: In this paper, "advisor" refers broadly to licensed agents, financial professionals, and RIAs unless otherwise noted.

Strategy Overview: Bring UX In-House, Digitizing & Infusing AI

To realize this vision, our strategy centers on reclaiming full in-house ownership of the user experience—designing it end-to-end, digitizing every operational and service interaction—from onboarding and suitability to servicing, retention, and support—and augmenting it with AI to deliver speed, personalization, and measurable outcomes. Instead of patchwork solutions or third-party portals, we will build a unified platform for our agents, advisors, and their clients—effectively creating our own "digital storefront" for annuities and retirement products. This will consist of role-specific experiences (advisor portal, client mobile app, RIA access, plan sponsor dashboard), all built on a shared platform and unified data model. Behind the scenes, we will integrate with best-in-class processing engines (Zinnia, SS&C) via APIs to handle policy administration and record-keeping, so we gain agility without reinventing core systems. On top of this foundation, we will embed AI throughout the customer journey to provide smart, personalized, and proactive services that distinguish us from traditional insurers. Data will be unified enterprise-wide to fuel these AI capabilities and analytics-driven decision making. This technology shift—driven by emerging capabilities like GenAI and intelligent automation—will be accelerated by the agile operating model we already have in place. This existing operating structure positions us to rapidly integrate these innovations across teams, processes, and partners to deliver measurable outcomes. Finally, our strategy aligns with where the industry is headed, positioning us competitively for the future.

Below, we break down the strategic pillars:

1. Own the Customer Experience via a Unified Digital Platform

Take direct control of the user experience by developing a single, unified digital platform for all interactions. This means agents, financial advisors, and their clients will use our in-house portal/app for sales, onboarding, servicing, and account management – rather than disparate third-party systems or clunky admin interfaces. By owning the front-end, we ensure the experience is modern, intuitive, and consistently reflects our brand values.

  • Unified Self-Service Portals: We will provide role-based self-service dashboards (for advisors/agents, for plan sponsors, for clients) that offer end-to-end visibility into annuity and retirement products. For example, an advisor can submit new business, track case status in real time, view commission statements, and service clients' policies all in one place. Similarly, clients can log in though the portal or a our mobile app to view contract values, make service requests, or explore new product offerings. A single sign-on and interface improves convenience and transparency for all users. Zinnia's approach validates this: through API-enabled components their platform "enables all personas to have total transparency at any given time about the state of their policy" and real-time self-service, while also reducing operational costs by deflecting calls.
  • Seamless Omnichannel Experience: Owning the platform allows us to create a consistent experience across channels – web, mobile, and even phone support. Users could start a transaction or inquiry online and later get help from an agent who sees the same context, without restarting the process. This cross-channel continuity addresses a common pain point: customers hate repeating information. Leading insurers use AI to ensure if a conversation starts on a mobile app and continues by phone, the context carries over so the customer isn't asked to re-explain. We will enable similar continuity, which improves satisfaction and trust. The platform will also support multiple interaction modes (text chat, voice commands, visual dashboards) to cater to different preferences – aligning with how consumer tech has shaped expectations .
  • Integrated Workflows and Straight-Through Processing: By owning the front-end experience and orchestrating processes via APIs, we can implement true Straight-Through Processing (STP) for critical transactions. Data entered by an advisor or client in the portal will flow directly into back-end systems—such as policy issuance, transfers, or withdrawals—without manual re-keying or handoffs. This not only reduces error but dramatically accelerates cycle times. The industry is already moving in this direction: for example, the Insured Retirement Institute's (IRI) digital standards have demonstrated they can reduce annuity transfer times from an average of 18 days to under 24 hours. Our goal is to deliver similarly "instant" issuance and servicing—by design. With Can-Sell checks shifted to the beginning of the new business process and Suitability validation fully automated, Not-In-Good-Order (NIGO) rates will drop sharply. The platform will validate inputs in real time, flag errors or omissions immediately, and ensure that all required data is captured before submission. As IRI has noted, STP eliminates the human error that leads to NIGO applications. In short, by owning the experience layer, we can architect digital journeys that are accurate, compliant, and efficient by default—reducing friction for advisors and clients, while accelerating outcomes.
  • Enhanced Onboarding for Agents and Clients: A unified digital onboarding process will make it fast and easy for new stakeholders to begin doing business with us. For agents and advisors, we will streamline contracting, licensing, and training through an online onboarding module – potentially cutting the onboarding time from the typical 2–3 weeks down to a matter of days with automation . New agents can upload documents, e-sign contracts, and complete necessary compliance training all on the platform, with status tracking and AI assistance (discussed further below). For clients purchasing annuities, the platform will guide them (or their advisor) through the application steps with e-signatures, built-in suitability checks, and instant feedback if something is missing. We could add support for onboarding of RIA's reducing onboarding time from 7 days to less than 30mins. By simplifying onboarding, we remove friction from distribution, accelerate revenue, and create an experience that strengthens both agent loyalty and client trust.

In summary, bringing the experience in-house via a unified platform gives us agility to continuously improve the UI/UX, implement industry best practices quickly, and differentiate on service. It turns our digital interface into a competitive asset. Moreover, it positions us to capture rich user data (every click, every query) that we can feed into our analytics – something we can't easily do when the experience sits with an external vendor. Finally, this platform-centric approach aligns with the broader industry direction: retirement and insurance providers are increasingly focusing on digital front-ends to deliver superior experience across all stakeholder groups . We intend to lead on this front.

2. Leverage Zinnia & SS&C as Back-End Engines via API Integration

While we revolutionize the front-end, we will smartly leverage our partners Zinnia and SS&C on the back-end. These systems will serve as our "engines" for policy administration, record-keeping, and transactions – accessed through modern APIs. The strategy is to avoid rebuilding what already works at scale (e.g. policy management, event processing, payment processing) and instead integrate those capabilities into our unified platform. By doing so, we gain the best of both worlds: a custom digital experience with agility to innovate, and robust back-office processing that is reliable and compliant.

  • API-First Integration: We will work with Zinnia and SS&C to interface via their published APIs and services. Both partners support this approach – for instance, Zinnia explicitly offers an API integration layer enabling access to a comprehensive library of life and annuity services. This means common tasks (submitting an application, running an illustration, checking contract values, processing a withdrawal, etc.) can be executed by our platform calling Zinnia/SS&C APIs behind the scenes. The user sees a seamless workflow on our front-end, while the heavy lifting is done by the back-end system processing the request. Real-time data exchange replaces what might otherwise be batch file uploads. As Katherine Dease of IRI noted during the "Digital First" initiative: we must abandon "big, flat files" and provide "access to data right now, real time" to meet client and advisor expectations. API integration is how we achieve that real-time responsiveness.
  • Orchestrated, Not Isolated, Processes: Using APIs, we can orchestrate end-to-end processes that span systems, without exposing the complexity to the user. For example, a new annuity application involves a suitability check (done using Zinnia Suitability-as-a-Service), a background check on the client (via a third-party service), issuing a policy number (in Zinnia's Zahara), and setting up payment. Our platform can call each service/API in sequence and display a unified progress to the user. From the user's perspective, it's one smooth workflow on one system. Internally, we handle the integrations and any exceptions. This also allows for mixing and matching services – e.g., if we want to plug in a new AI-driven model alongside Zinnia's engine, we could do so via APIs without disrupting the front-end. The overall architecture becomes modular and flexible, in line with modern best practices for an AI-enabled insurer. McKinsey calls this a "composable, distributed architectural paradigm" where multiple agents/systems collaborate via secure APIs .
  • Zinnia for Life & Annuity Core: Zinnia will be our powerhouse for annuity product administration. It is built to handle new business processing, policy servicing, and compliance for life insurance and annuities at scale. By integrating Zinnia, we tap into a platform that is already modernizing with a data-centric, cloud-native approach . Zinnia's vision to simplify insurance through technology means we can expect continuous improvements from their side (AI, blockchain for ledger, etc.). For instance, Zinnia has deployed AI-powered call center capabilities to boost productivity - capabilities we might leverage via API for our own service center. In short, Zinnia provides a robust core system of record and transaction processing, and we provide the experience layer. This division of labor accelerates our timeline (no need to convert to a new policy admin system) and lowers risk, while still letting us differentiate on experience.
  • SS&C for Retirement Plan Administration: SS&C will serve as our engine for 403(b) and other retirement plan operations. SS&C Retirement Solutions is positioned as a "single source for high-quality, end-to-end solutions connecting the retirement ecosystem" . SS&C can handle everything from enrollment, contributions, compliance testing, to distributions for retirement plans. We will integrate these capabilities so that, for example, a plan sponsor or participant using our platform can view their account or initiate a transaction, which our system will execute by calling SS&C in the background. The comprehensive nature of SS&C's platform means we can support complex operations (like plan rollovers or loan processing) by utilizing their APIs rather than building that logic from scratch. Importantly, both Zinnia and SS&C integrations will feed into our unified data store, ensuring that data from those systems is aggregated for a single customer view.
  • Contact Center Reimagination: Building on our unified portal and AI stack, we will bring Zinnia's and SS&C's contact center operations fully in-house—not as a traditional live call center, but as a digital-first, AI-powered service experience. By default, routine advisor and client queries—such as policy status, beneficiary updates, or withdrawal requests—will be handled via automated chat or AI-enhanced IVR. This allows us to deflect 60–75% of calls through intelligent self-service. When escalation is needed, calls will route seamlessly to internal trained agents who receive AI-generated summaries, sentiment cues, and co-pilot recommendations to accelerate resolution. BPO experiences with AI-powered call centers have shown up to 70% productivity gains, demonstrating the operational upside of this approach . The end result: faster service, fewer errors, and vastly improved experience for both advisors and clients.
  • Straight-Through Processing & Automation via APIs: By using APIs to tie our front-end to back-ends, we achieve true Straight-Through Processing. A digital application completed on our platform triggers API calls that result in an issued policy, often without any manual review unless something falls out of bounds. This was previously mentioned, but it's worth reinforcing: the C2C (carrier-to-carrier) annuity replacement pilot achieved 48–72 hour processing by automating steps that used to be manual. Athene's experience (scaling from 5% to 90% electronic submissions) underscores that carriers who fully integrate and automate will handle volumes that are impossible via legacy methods . We plan to automate not just new business, but also in-force transactions (beneficiary changes, fund allocations, withdrawals, etc.) to be straight-through. Every manual touchpoint we eliminate reduces cost and error, and improves turnaround. Over time, this yields significant efficiency gains – the kind that drive unit cost down and scalability up. In fact, McKinsey notes that transforming entire domains with AI and automation can improve the bottom line by double digits, whereas isolated efforts cannot.

In summary, integrating Zinnia and SS&C via API allows us to concentrate our innovation at the experience layer while relying on proven systems for core processing. It's a strategic use of partnerships: keep commoditized or heavy-compliance functions in expert systems, and build proprietary advantage in how we deliver those functions to users. This approach mirrors a hybrid build/buy model, where we "buy" (or rather utilize) standardized back-end capabilities and "build" our unique front-end and AI features. Industry experts advocate this hybrid model to balance differentiation and cost-effectiveness . By focusing internal development on what truly differentiates (digital UX, data, AI) and leveraging partners for the rest, we accelerate our transformation and avoid creating a new legacy system that could bog us down. The result will be a unified yet flexible digital ecosystem: one front-door through which all interactions flow, powered by interconnected services underneath.

3. Embed AI Capabilities Across the Journey to Differentiate

Digital technology alone isn't enough – it's the intelligence we layer on top that will make our experience truly best-in-market. We plan to embed Artificial Intelligence at every critical touchpoint of the client and advisor journey. By doing so, we turn interactions that were once generic or manual into personalized, proactive, and smart experiences. AI will help us scale high-quality service without equivalent headcount growth, and delight users with capabilities that feel "a step ahead." Below are key AI capabilities we will implement:

  • AI Virtual Assistants (Chatbots & Voicebots): We will deploy intelligent virtual assistants to provide instant, 24/7 support for both advisors and customers. These AI bots – accessible via chat on our portal or via phone IVR – will handle routine inquiries and service requests conversationally. For example, an agent could ask the chatbot, "What's the status of my client's annuity application?" and get a quick answer, or a customer might query, "How do I update my beneficiaries?" and the bot can guide them through it. Today's consumers increasingly expect such AI-driven self-service; in fact, many already seek financial information or advice from AI tools like ChatGPT . Our virtual assistant will be trained on our products, forms, and policies, enabling it to resolve common questions (policy features, account balances, form help) and even complete transactions (e.g., "Initiate a withdrawal of $5,000"). This offloads workload from call centers and provides help instantaneously at any hour. A McKinsey study found that implementing a 24/7 chatbot boosted conversion of prospects – 11% more prospective customers ended up buying policies after the insurer added an after-hours chatbot . We anticipate similar benefits: more inquiries converted to sales, and higher customer satisfaction due to immediate response. Importantly, our bots will be designed for smooth handoff to human agents when queries get complex – including transferring the full context of the interaction so the user isn't frustrated. Over time, as the AI learns from interactions, it will handle a growing share of inquiries (industry data suggests up to 80% of routine queries could be handled by AI agents), and do so with consistency and accuracy that builds trust.
  • AI‑Powered Digital Contact Center (Inbound): Facilities like chat and AI‑enhanced IVR resolve many advisor and customer inquiries in real time—policy status, payout requests, transfer windows, and more. Calls that require agent intervention escalate with intelligent summaries, sentiment scores, and co‑pilot suggestions for swift, compliant resolution.
  • AI-Powered Customer Onboarding & Service: We will inject AI into the onboarding processes for both new clients and new advisors to create a frictionless start. On the client side, this means using AI to auto-fill or validate application data, assess suitability (including financial status) in real-time by comparing inputs against product rules (minimum amount, rider review), assess risks and monitor for fraudulent behavior in real-time. McKinsey predicts that in the near future, nearly all customer onboarding steps could be handled by AI "multi-agent" systems – one agent gathering info and clarifying with the customer, another evaluating risk, another checking compliance, etc., all working in concert. We aim to evolve toward that vision, starting with simpler components like an "intake AI" that guides the user through forms with dynamic help. For example, if an applicant's answer triggers a potential issue (say an age out of range for a product), the AI can immediately flag it or suggest an alternative product, rather than waiting days for a back-office review and then reject it. This proactive guidance will significantly reduce cycle time and NIGO applications, as errors are caught and corrected on the spot. For advisor onboarding (agent contracting), an AI assistant can streamline background checks, training and paperwork (e.g. collect Tax ID & W9). We will integrate with databases (e.g. NIPR licensing registry) to instantly verify licenses or credentials and generate state notification of appointment & fees for review by a service agent. The AI could also personalize the onboarding journey: if it notices, for instance, that an agent is not appointed in a particular state, it can prompt the necessary steps. The result is a faster, smoother onboarding for our distribution partners, which makes us easier to do business with compared to competitors. Post-onboarding, AI will continue to enhance service. Consider claims or distributions – traditionally stressful moments. An AI could walk a beneficiary through a death claim notification, empathetically collect necessary data, and automatically submit required forms, only alerting a human if something unusual arises. By embedding AI in these service moments, we ensure even complex transactions are handled with speed and accuracy, and with a human-like touch when needed. We will replicate such "AI copilot" support in servicing, where the AI might suggest to a service agent the next best response or auto-fill forms during a call, minimizing hold times and errors.
  • Personalized Product Recommendations (Next-Best-Action): With our unified data and AI algorithms, we will be able to analyze each customer's profile and behavior to offer tailored recommendations that improve outcomes for them and sales for us. For example, an advisor using our platform could receive an AI-generated insight: "Client X has a large cash position – consider discussing a fixed indexed annuity to protect and grow those assets." Or a plan participant might get a nudge on their portal like: "Based on your age fifty-five and risk profile, increasing contributions by Y% or considering an in-plan annuity could strengthen your retirement income." These recommendations will be driven by machine learning models that consider hundreds of data points (age, account balance, life events, market conditions, prior interactions, etc.). Next-best-action engines have become a key tool in insurance and banking to drive upsell and retention. In our case, by leveraging data across annuity and retirement products, we can identify needs that a human advisor might overlook. This not only boosts cross-sell (e.g., suggesting a long-term care rider or an IRA rollover opportunity) but also enhances the customer's experience – they feel understood and serviced proactively rather than sold to. Notably, predictive models have been shown to significantly improve cross-selling campaigns, yielding higher sales and customer satisfaction . Our aim is to use AI to present the right solution at the right time for each client, thereby improving their financial outcomes (for instance, ensuring they don't miss a chance to secure lifetime income or protect against a risk) and concurrently driving our growth.
  • Advisor "Co-Pilot" Tools: Instead of viewing AI as replacing human advisors, we will position it as a co-pilot to enhance their effectiveness. For our wholesalers, call center reps, and the financial advisors who distribute our products, we will provide AI-driven tools that act like a smart assistant always by their side. One such tool could be an AI knowledge assistant: advisors can query it in natural language for complex product info, comparisons, or policy rules and get instant, accurate answers (sourced from our product manuals, prospectuses, and regulations). This saves them time flipping through documents and ensures they give clients correct information. We will build an integrated product repository with AI search that allows call center agents to seamlessly answer detailed coverage questions on the fly—demonstrating how AI can elevate service quality with quick, context-rich answers. We will implement a similar knowledge platform for annuity features, rider details, IRS rules for 403(b), etc., accessible to agents and internal staff. Another tool could be an Automated Meeting Brief Assistant. Before a scheduled advisor-client meeting, the Advisor Co-Pilot generates a smart pre-meeting brief, pulling from CRM data, account history, portfolios, and client notes. It includes tailored talking points, client milestones, and recommended next-best-actions. This saves advisors preparation time and ensures each engagement is informed, consistent, and outcome-oriented. Furthermore, AI can assist advisors in proposal generation. By inputting a client's goals and constraints, an advisor could have the AI draft a personalized annuity proposal or retirement plan, backed by data and compliant language. This draft can then be reviewed and refined by the advisor, cutting proposal prep time dramatically. It essentially allows advisors to do more with less, focusing their expertise where it matters (relationship-building and complex advice) while the AI handles research and paperwork. By offering these co-pilot tools, we strengthen loyalty with our distribution partners – we become known as the carrier that makes advisors' lives easier and helps them succeed, rather than just pushing products.
  • Process Automation & Operational AI: Beyond customer-facing uses, we will deploy AI to streamline internal and BPO operations. This includes using Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to automate many back-office tasks that are still manual today. For instance, we can use AI-driven document processing to handle incoming paperwork or email requests. An AI could scan an email from an advisor, interpret it ("client wants to change beneficiaries"), and automatically route it into the right workflow or even complete the change if proper authorization is detected. This reduces the workload on operations teams and speeds up turnaround. We will also implement Robotic Process Automation (RPA) bots for repetitive tasks such as data entry between legacy systems (if any remain outside our unified platform) or reconciliation tasks. However, our approach goes beyond basic RPA – by incorporating ML, the bots can handle variability and learn from exceptions over time, making them more resilient than traditional scripts. Moreover, we'll leverage AI in areas like fraud detection and compliance monitoring (e.g. Socure). Machine learning models can flag unusual withdrawal patterns that might indicate fraud or elder financial abuse, alerting our compliance team to intervene early. Similarly, AI can screen transactions for compliance with regulations (e.g. IRS contribution limits on retirement plans) automatically, preventing errors that could incur penalties. Another operational area is customer behavior analytics: AI models can predict which policyholders may be at risk of surrendering their annuity or which plan participants might roll over funds out of our platform, enabling our teams to take proactive action (special offers, personal outreach). Studies show AI-driven retention strategies can significantly reduce churn – by as much as 45% reduction in attrition according to some industry analyses . This ties into our data analytics pillar (next section) but underscores that AI will permeate not just front-end interactions but also the back-end decision-making that keeps our business running efficiently.

In all these applications, a guiding principle is that AI should enhance human capability and customer experience, not just cut costs. We'll measure success not only by efficiency gains (though those are important) but also by customer-centric metrics: faster response times, higher Net Promoter Scores, lower error rates, and greater personalization as evidenced by improved conversion and retention. By embedding AI throughout the journey, we differentiate our service as smart and proactive. Insurers that deeply integrate AI "in everything they do" will conduct business faster and more personally, leaving dabblers behind . Our goal is to be firmly in that leader category – using AI pervasively to deliver an experience that feels not just digital, but truly intelligent and tailored for each user.

4. Data Unification and Analytics

Underpinning our digital platform and AI initiatives is a unified, high-quality data foundation - (ACUMEN). Data is often called the lifeblood of AI – all AI runs on data – and it's also crucial for us to make informed business decisions and personalized customer interactions. In our current state, data resides in silos (annuity admin system, separate 403b recordkeeper, CRM systems, CDR/CDP, PeopleSoft, FAR etc.), limiting our ability to glean insights. We receive data from partners like Zinnia and SS&C via thousands of batch files. This strategy will establish a unified data platform that receives data via a 'data sharing' capability and consolidates information across all products and touchpoints, enabling advanced analytics and a "single source of truth" for each customer and distributor.

  • 360° View of Customers and Partners: We will integrate data from Zinnia, SS&C, our CRM, marketing channels, and the new digital platform into a central data repository (data lake/warehouse) we call ACUMEN—our unified platform for enterprise analytics and machine learning. This unified dataset will give us a comprehensive view of each customer (e.g., demographic info, all policies/accounts held, transaction history, communication logs, web interactions) as well as each advisor/agent (licenses, production, service history, feedback, etc.). With this 360° view, our AI and analytics models can be far more effective. For example, an analytics model could correlate how portal usage patterns relate to persistency – perhaps finding that customers who haven't logged in for 6 months are at higher lapse risk. We could then intervene with targeted engagement for those customers. Or we might discover certain advisors have much faster cycle times, and correlate that with their usage of digital tools, then propagate those best practices to other advisors. Such insights are only possible with unified data across the journey.
  • Modern Data Infrastructure: We will leverage cloud-based data storage and processing to handle the volume and variety of data in real-time. Streams of data from user interactions (clicks, chatbot conversations), transactional data from back-ends, and external data (market data, socio-economic data for customers, etc.) will all feed into our platform. Investment in data engineering and governance is critical – we need to ensure data quality, consistency (common definitions for fields), and compliance (proper handling of PII and sensitive info). As McKinsey notes, insurers will need to fundamentally enhance their data capabilities to achieve their AI vision . This includes data cleaning, establishing data lineage, and possibly creating data domains aligned with business areas (e.g. a "customer domain", "policy domain") managed by accountable owners. By doing this groundwork, we make data accessible and trustworthy across the enterprise.
  • Analytics and BI for Decision-Making: With unified data, we can empower our teams with better Business Intelligence (BI) and analytics. Dashboards will track key metrics in real-time: sales funnel conversion rates, turnaround times, NIGO rates, customer satisfaction scores, etc., sliced by product, channel, and team. This gives leadership a pulse on the business and the impact of our improvements. We'll adopt a test-and-learn mentality: for instance, if we launch a new AI tool, we will measure its effect (did chatbots reduce call volume? did STP increase issue rate?). The data platform will let us detect these trends quickly and iterate. Essentially, data will drive continuous improvement in both the customer experience and internal operations.
  • Advanced Analytics and AI Modeling: Beyond descriptive BI, we'll apply advanced analytics (predictive and prescriptive models) to drive outcomes:
    • Sales & Marketing Analytics: Using historical data, our propensity models can identify which agents are more likely to covert to selling our products, as well as which agents are our most desired targets. Propensity models could also identify which leads or prospects are most likely to purchase an annuity, and focus advisor outreach accordingly. We can also analyze advisor behavior to predict who might be drifting (e.g., an advisor who hasn't written business in a while might be wooed by competitors) and engage them with support or incentives before we lose them.
    • Cross-Sell and Upsell Models: As mentioned earlier, a next-best-offer model can suggest additional products to existing clients. By mining our data, we might find, for example, that clients who own a deferred annuity for 5+ years and are over 60 are highly likely to consider an income annuity – that insight can trigger targeted marketing. Insurers have found that such data-driven cross-selling leads to significant uplift in sales, improving not just revenue but also customer retention (since multi-product customers stay longer).
    • Churn and Retention Modeling: We will implement models to predict lapses, surrenders, or plan turnover. Variables may include customer demographics, service frequencies, market conditions, etc. This allows us to proactively intervene (e.g., if a model flags that a particular contract is at high risk of surrender, we can have a retention specialist or the advisor reach out with a personalized offer or conversation). The importance of retention cannot be overstated: acquiring a new customer costs many times more than retaining one, and loyal customers generate significantly more lifetime value . Analytics can dramatically improve retention – even a few percentage points improvement is valuable. There is evidence that leveraging AI and predictive analytics can boost retention rates by 10-15% or more in insurance by enabling targeted retention actions .
    • Operational Analytics: We'll use data to optimize our processes as well. For instance, analyzing which steps in the application process see the most drop-offs or errors can pinpoint where to simplify the UI or add AI help. Or monitoring processing times by product and distributor can highlight bottlenecks to address (maybe a particular check takes too long – we could then automate it).
  • AI Model Development and Intellectual Property: Over time, our accumulation of unified data and modeling experience can become a competitive moat. We will develop proprietary AI models (for risk scoring, customer segmentation, etc.) that incorporate our unique insights and data patterns. BCG notes that embedding an insurer's "special sauce" (e.g., unique underwriting know-how or distribution insights) into AI systems can become part of its intellectual property. For example, our data might reveal patterns about advisor behavior that correlate with quality business – we could train an AI to identify and recruit high-potential advisors accordingly, something competitors without our data might not replicate. Additionally, we will structure our AI development to reuse components – if we build a document classification AI for new business, we can reuse it for classifying incoming service requests, etc., to maximize ROI .

In conclusion, unifying data and leveraging analytics turns our information into strategic advantage. It enables personalization at scale (feeding the AI in pillar 3), drives smarter business decisions, and creates feedback loops to constantly refine our strategy. Companies that have embraced advanced analytics in insurance report impressive results – one life insurance study found predictive analytics led to a 67% drop in expenses and 60% boost in sales . While those figures encompass many improvements, it illustrates the magnitude of impact when data is harnessed effectively. Our aim is to build a data-driven culture where intuition is supported by insights, and where data flows freely but securely to where it's needed. This pillar also supports regulatory compliance and risk management – with better data lineage and quality, we can respond faster to audits or regulation changes (e.g., understanding our exposure if interest rates move, by querying one data source). In sum, data is the foundation that will both power our AI and inform our human strategy, ensuring we execute with precision and learn continuously.

5. Operational Alignment and Change Management

Implementing the above technological changes requires an agile operating model and culture (this is our Customer Centric Delivery Model - CCDM). To truly become an AI-driven, digitally focused enterprise, we must continue to rewire how our teams work, how we manage initiatives, and how we support our people through change. Many digital transformations fail not due to tech, but due to organizational resistance or misalignment. We will avoid that by continuing to align our operations to this vision:

  • Domain-Focused, Agile Operating Model: Rather than organizing work purely by function (IT, Operations, Sales, Marketing etc.), we will adopt a domain-based approach. A domain is a business-aligned functional area organized around a set of customer or advisor outcomes. For example, Advisor Onboarding & Enablement, Customer & Advisor Support, Cross-Sell & Retention, Digital Experience & Engagement. This means forming cross-functional teams that include the appropriate mix of software developers, product managers, operations specialists, distribution/sales experts, brand/marketing liaisons, data scientists and data analysts all working together on delivering the annuity & retirement digital experience. McKinsey emphasizes selecting an operating model that supports AI at scale – an enterprise-wide agile model – to break down silos and accelerate innovation . By having all necessary skills in one team, aligned to common KPIs, we ensure that when the team develops a new feature (say a Next-Best-Action model or a chatbot script), it's done with input from all stakeholders and with end-to-end ownership. This also allows faster iteration – the team can release improvements in sprints (e.g., every 2 weeks), test them with users, gather feedback (from data analytics), and adjust continuously.
  • Robust Product Management and Vision Ownership: We will empower strong product managers/owners for key components of the experience – e.g., one for the advisor portal, one for the customer mobile app, one for AI assistant capabilities. These PMs will act as the "CEO" of their product, balancing business objectives, user experience, and technical feasibility. Deloitte highlights robust product management as a crucial factor for transformation success . These product owners ensure that development efforts stay aligned with the strategic vision and deliver real value, rather than getting lost in technical minutiae. They also prioritize features based on impact (informed by data). For instance, a product owner might decide that reducing NIGO in the application flow is top priority for next quarter, and rally the team to implement additional AI validation checks to achieve that. By clearly defining product roadmaps and measuring outcomes (e.g., NIGO rate reduction), we maintain a value-driven approach.
  • AI Control Tower and Governance: The AI Control Tower is a cross-functional governance model designed to ensure AI use across the organization is strategic, ethical, and value-generating. It includes:
    • The AI Ethics Working Group, which safeguards compliance, fairness, and ethical use of AI;
    • The AI Solutions Working Group, which identifies and prioritizes high-value, transformative use cases;
    • NextGen Labs, whose members build and deliver AI solutions across business domains.
    Operating under the Generative AI Usage Policy, the AI Control Tower provides unified oversight, streamlines decision-making, and fosters collaboration to maximize the business value of AI while minimizing risk.
  • Training and Upskilling: We recognize that our workforce (and partner workforce in BPOs or distribution) needs new skills to thrive in this digital model. We will invest in comprehensive training programs to upskill employees on the new tools and processes. This includes technical training (e.g., how to interpret analytics dashboards, how to use the new CRM or portal features) and soft skills (e.g., how to partner effectively in agile teams, how to manage change). For advisors and operations users, we'll provide training on using the platform and AI assistants to augment their work. The goal is to reduce fear and build confidence that AI is a tool for them, not a threat. In parallel, we'll nurture data literacy and AI literacy across the organization – helping people at all levels understand at a conceptual level what these technologies do and how to leverage them. When employees understand the "why" and "how" of these changes, they are far more likely to adopt them enthusiastically.
  • Change Management and Adoption Drive: As a rule of thumb, for every dollar or hour we invest in technology, we should invest an equal amount in driving adoption and change management. This means dedicating resources to communication, training (as above), feedback collection, and iterative improvement. We will involve end-users early by getting their input on designs and running pilot programs. Early wins will be publicized internally to build momentum (for example, highlighting a story of an advisor who used our new portal and improved her productivity, or an ops team that eliminated a backlog thanks to AI assistance). We'll also identify and support change champions – influential staff or advisors who can evangelize the new ways of working to their peers. Metrics will be put in place to measure adoption (e.g., % of applications submitted via the digital portal vs old channels, number of chatbot interactions vs calls, etc.), and teams will be accountable for not just building the tool, but ensuring it's used. If an AI tool is delivering suboptimal results or not being embraced, our culture will encourage us to dig in and improve it, rather than blame the users or abandon the tool . One sign of true adoption is when employees view AI assistants as integral to their jobs, to the point they take ownership of improving those AI tools (e.g., an RVP providing feedback to refine an AI model's decisions). We intend to cultivate that mindset.
  • Alignment with BPO Partners and Vendors: Our Business Process Outsourcing partners (for contact center or policy processing) must be part of this transition—particularly as we bring the contact center function fully in-house. While BPO partners will continue to support select operational processes during the transition, we will staff and train our own internal service teams, fully aligned to our unified platform and AI-assisted workflows. These internal agents will deliver a digitally native service experience—guided by AI co-pilots, working within our experience layer, and measured by outcomes like digital resolution rates, first-contact containment, and customer satisfaction, not traditional call volumes. During the transition, we will extend change management and tooling alignment to our BPO partners where appropriate, ensuring no disruption to customers and consistent service standards across the hybrid model. Contracts with existing vendors may be restructured to reflect evolving responsibilities and introduce KPIs tied to digital workflow participation and self-service enablement. Ultimately, we will reduce our dependency on external contact centers and ensure that all customer service—regardless of channel—is orchestrated under our control, reinforcing a single, intelligent, omnichannel experience. By aligning staffing, tooling, and metrics to our digital-first strategy, we avoid fragmentation and deliver consistent, high-quality outcomes for agents and clients alike.
  • Leadership and Cultural Shift: Leadership will visibly champion this change. This includes communicating a clear vision (as in this document) that explains why we are doing this – not just to save costs, but to deliver superior customer outcomes and ensure our long-term competitiveness. We'll encourage a culture that embraces experimentation (try new AI tools in a controlled way) and accepts that not everything will succeed on the first try (the idea of "fail fast, learn, and iterate"). Employees should feel safe providing feedback on the new system and suggesting improvements – frontline insights are invaluable. We'll celebrate not just the end results but also the behaviors we want (e.g., an associate using data to make a decision, or an advisor group that migrated fully to the new platform). Over time, our culture should evolve to one that is data-driven, customer-centric, and agile – comfortable with continuous change as the new normal. This cultural transformation is perhaps the hardest part, but absolutely crucial. As noted in an industry analysis, change management can account for half of the effort in AI transformations, underscoring that technology is only half the battle.

By aligning our operating model to these principles, we create an environment where the technology solutions can actually deliver their potential value. If we didn't change how we work, the fancy new platform or AI might languish unused or not fully integrated – a fate that has beset many past tech projects in insurance. We refuse to let that happen. We will have the structure, roles, and incentives in place so that digital and AI are at the core of everyone's job, not a side project. When an underwriter, a service rep, and a software developer work together daily on a common goal (say, speeding up issuance), and they have the tools and mandate to do so, remarkable innovation can occur. This operational alignment ensures our transformation is sustainable, not just a one-off effort. It also builds organizational agility that will serve us beyond this initiative – we'll be better equipped to tackle future changes (like new regulations or emerging technologies) because we've built a muscle for cross-functional collaboration and rapid adaptation.

6. Alignment with Industry Future & Competitive Positioning

Our strategy is not developed in a vacuum; it intentionally aligns with the future direction of the industry and positions us ahead of competitors. By executing this plan, we will meet the rising expectations of consumers and distribution partners, comply with evolving industry standards, and capitalize on the momentum of technological change in insurance. Here's how this strategy fits into the broader landscape:

  • Meeting Evolving Customer Expectations: Consumers today compare their financial service experiences to their best experiences in other industries. Thanks to AI-driven services in e-commerce and banking, customers now expect accuracy, speed, and personalization in insurance as well. They are increasingly open to interacting with AI (over a third of GenAI users already seek financial advice from such tools). By providing human-like chatbots, instant processing, and tailored recommendations, we match those expectations. Advisors, too, are using sophisticated tools in wealth management – if we don't give them a cutting-edge platform for annuities, we risk seeming archaic. Our strategy ensures we're not just keeping up, but setting a new bar for convenience and intelligence in annuity distribution. This will resonate especially with younger advisors and clients who are digital natives.
  • Leveraging Industry Initiatives (Digital Standards): The industry is collectively pushing for modernization, as seen with initiatives like IRI's "Digital First for Annuities" and the Carrier Integration Hub with DTCC. By moving to straight-through-processing and digital workflows, we align with these initiatives and can be an active participant. We noted earlier that IRI anticipates a large number of carriers joining the digital replacement platform in 2025/26 . We want to be among the leaders, not laggards, in that coalition. This not only improves our operations but also our reputation in the distribution community (as a forward-thinking partner). When we meet standards (for data exchange, electronic forms, etc.), we make it easier for distributors to do business with us, potentially attracting more shelf space and recommendations from IMOs and broker-dealers. Being a leader here could also allow us to help shape industry standards to our benefit.
  • Outpacing Competitors through AI-Leadership: Studies have started to quantify the advantage AI leaders have over peers. In insurance, the top AI-enabled companies have achieved over 6 times higher total shareholder returns in the past five years compared to AI laggards. This is a staggering difference, and it illustrates a clear future: those who embrace AI and digital will capture market share and financial gains at the expense of those who do not. By executing our AI-driven strategy now, we aim to join those leader ranks. We've seen concrete examples: insurers like Aviva are deploying dozens of AI models and saving tens of millions of dollars, or insurers who automated sales saw 80% transactions move online and big jumps in customer referral scores . We are positioning our company to achieve similar or greater benefits. Our differentiation will be especially visible in distribution: advisors might start preferring to sell our annuities because the process is so much faster and simpler, which in turn drives our sales growth. In contrast, if we stuck to business-as-usual, we could be "left in the dust" as McKinsey warns , unable to keep up with AI-native competitors or new fintech entrants.
  • Future-Proofing for Emerging Tech and Regulations: The strategy builds a flexible, modular architecture (thanks to APIs and a modern stack) that will allow us to plug in future technologies, such as more advanced AI (GPT-5, new Open Source LLMs, etc.), blockchain for secure records, or open data integration as they become relevant for insurance. We are effectively creating an AI-ready infrastructure that can evolve. This also helps with regulatory agility – for instance, if regulations demand more transparency in AI decisions, our AI governance and data lineage will have us prepared to explain and adjust models as needed. If a new cybersecurity standard emerges, our cloud-based, well-architected platform can implement it faster than legacy systems could. In essence, we're not just solving today's problems but building capabilities for the next decade of innovation.
  • Enhancing Distribution Partner Relationships: By focusing on digital enablement, we are also addressing the top frustrations of financial professionals in selling annuities. LIMRA research consistently shows the #1 obstacle for advisors is cumbersome paperwork and slow processes. By eliminating those pain points, we make ourselves the "easy button" for annuities. This can be a huge competitive edge in a space where product differences can be small – ease of doing business becomes a deciding factor for advisors choosing between carriers. We expect to see higher advisor loyalty, more firms including us on their recommended lists, and possibly the ability to onboard new distribution channels (like Registered Investment Advisors) who previously avoided annuities due to complexity. Additionally, our platform could enable new partnership models, such as embedding our annuities into fintech marketplaces via APIs (since we'll have the digital chops to do so), further extending our reach.
  • Focusing on Customer Outcomes to Differentiate Brand: Strategically, our vision of delivering outcomes (e.g. helping customers secure retirement income easily, helping advisors grow their business) can become a brand differentiator. Many insurance companies still talk in terms of products and features. We can stand out by marketing the experience and outcomes – "With us, your annuity purchase is quick, transparent, and tailored to your needs; your retirement plan is always at your fingertips and in good hands." This resonates emotionally with customers and partners. It's a shift from being a product manufacturer to a solutions provider. In the long run, as more players digitize, those who truly organize around customer outcomes will win. We are structuring our entire strategy around that principle (from platform design to AI use cases all focused on making the customer/advisor successful).
  • Competitive Benchmarking: It's worth noting that some competitors are moving in this direction – for instance, large life insurers have announced digital experience overhauls, and startups are trying to simplify annuities. But very few have comprehensively combined a unified platform, deep API integration, and AI infusion as we are planning. We aim to leapfrog piecemeal efforts by doing this holistically. When we're fully operational with our new model, the difference will be stark: what takes others weeks takes us hours; what confuses others' customers is straightforward for ours; what costs others a lot to process, we handle at a fraction of the cost due to automation. This efficiency can translate into more competitive pricing or higher payouts for our products, a virtuous cycle (better experience and better value).

In summary, this strategy doesn't just catch us up to the present – it propels us into the future state of the industry, where digital, data, and AI-driven service is the norm. We will be among the leaders setting that norm. The investments and changes we make are aligned with where regulators, industry bodies, and customer trends are all pointing. By acting now, we avoid playing catch-up later or losing relevance. Instead, we can capture first-mover advantages in certain areas and be well-positioned to adapt to whatever comes next (be it new fintech disruptors or shifts in customer demographics). As one executive said about the digitization of annuities, "Complacency is no longer an option" – our plan is the opposite of complacency; it's bold and proactive. And by executing it, we expect to earn recognition as an innovative, customer-first leader in annuity and retirement services, which will ultimately drive growth, profitability, and stakeholder value.
